Why Apply FAC?

As technology progresses, coding becomes more valuable to the overall body knowledge. Seeing the progress from not knowing any codes to erudition on the fundamental of HTML, CSS and Javascript. Including creating a website!

It has allowed me to show my peers that you can achieve anything as long you put work into it, believe and do not be afraid to ask for help.

With the experience working in a fintech company, I have discovered an interest in a community called Women In Tech.

What is Women In Tech?

It's a community that brings women together to socialise and share experience in the tech industry. I am so glad to encounter such community as we are aware currently tech industry has disproportionately white, male and university-educated.

Not saying it's a bad thing, however, as a woman being surrounded just with men in the office can feel pretty intimidating. Or being a minority can feel less heard.

Henceforth, why it's inspiring and motivating to be able to be a part of a community in changing the current scene in tech into a more diverse and opportunistic industry because it is!!!

Due to the unfortunate case of the pandemic, it has lead me to unemployment. Nevertheless, it is also a blessing in disguise as I was introduced by my recruiter to Founders & Coders. It has opened up my doors to programming and new career goals.

Through my journey in completing the prerequisite and learning to code, I've experienced different types of coaching, myself included as a coach, I've experienced that coaching can be a pleasure and frustration.

FAC has introduced and encouraged Peer Learning techniques.

What is that? "The term peer learning refers to situations where peers support each other in learning processes."
